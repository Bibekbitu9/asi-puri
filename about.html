About ASI Puri Circle

Dedicated to the preservation, conservation, and research of the rich archaeological heritage of the Odisha region.

Our History

The Archaeological Survey of India was established in 1861 by Alexander Cunningham, who became its first Director General. The Puri Circle was constituted to manage the exceptionally rich archaeological landscape of Odisha, home to some of India's finest temple architecture, Buddhist heritage sites, and ancient rock edicts.

Our Jurisdiction

The Puri Circle's jurisdiction extends across several districts of Odisha including Puri, Khordha, Cuttack, Jajpur, Ganjam, Kalahandi, Boudh, and neighbouring areas. The Circle manages over 80 centrally protected monuments and archaeological sites.

Our Mission

To protect, preserve, and present India's archaeological heritage through scientific conservation, rigorous research, and public engagement — fostering an appreciation for our shared cultural legacy.

Organizational Setup

ASI Puri Circle is headed by the Superintending Archaeologist, supported by Deputy Superintending Archaeologists, Assistant Archaeologists, Conservation Assistants, and administrative staff.
